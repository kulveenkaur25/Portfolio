Data-driven modeling to predict future energy demand and recommend policy interventions in extreme heat conditions.

Project Overview

In response to concerns from eSC, an energy provider in South Carolina, this project forecasts July electricity consumption under extreme heat scenarios. By analyzing weather and housing data, and building predictive models, we provide insights and recommendations to manage energy load more effectively.

Problem Statement

With global warming driving higher summer temperatures, eSC wants to prepare for energy surges. The goal is to predict demand during an ‚Äúextra-hot‚Äù July and offer mitigation strategies without expanding infrastructure.

Dataset & Sources

  • Static House Information (county, insulation, size, etc.)
  • Hourly energy consumption per household
  • Weather data per county (temperature, humidity)

Custom data pipeline was built to merge house, energy, and weather data with timezone adjustments and feature aggregation.

Data Pipeline & Preparation

  • Aggregated hourly energy consumption per county
  • Merged with hourly weather data (converted to EDT)
  • Created synthetic data with +5¬∞C temperature increase to simulate future scenarios

Modeling & Prediction
