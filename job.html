Project Overview

This Tableau project analyzes job market trends in the data science domain. It offers insights into global and U.S.-based job roles, salaries, and hiring patterns. The goal is to help iSchool students understand key roles, required skills, and top companies to make informed career decisions.

Dataset

We used job listing data collected and curated from online sources. The cleaned dataset was published here: https://tinyurl.com/projectdatasets

Interactive Dashboard Carousel

Overview Dashboard

This dashboard shows the global dominance of Data Scientist roles, illustrating the growing importance of data science in the modern workforce.

Dashboard 1: U.S. Job Landscape

The U.S. holds 63% of global data roles. Entry-level salaries start at $10K‚Äì$15K. Roles like ML Engineer & Data Analyst are in high demand.

Dashboard 2: Company Size & Salaries

Medium-sized companies dominate hiring (61%). Full-time roles are 99%. ML Engineers earn the highest average salaries.

Dashboard 3: Location-Based Analysis

California and Texas are top hiring states. Most salaries center around $100,000, with Data Engineers at the top of the pay scale.

Dashboard 4: Skills and Employers

Python, SQL, and Machine Learning are the most required skills. Employers like Alphabet and NVIDIA dominate hiring trends.

Observation & Conclusion

Data science remains a high-demand field. Roles such as Data Scientist and ML Engineer are growing rapidly, with salaries exceeding $100K. Skills like Python, SQL, and ML are crucial. Our dashboards provide actionable insights for students to align their learning paths with industry needs.
